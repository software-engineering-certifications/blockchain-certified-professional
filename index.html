What is Blockchain?

A blockchain is a distributed database that is dispersed among the nodes of a computer network. As a database, it stores an information electronically in digital format. Blockchains are best known for their crucial role in cryptocurrency systems, such as Bitcoin, for maintaining a secure, defensive and decentralized record of transactions. The innovation with a blockchain is that it guarantees the fidelity and security of a record of data will generates trust without the need of a trusted third party.

BLOCKCHAIN CERTIFIED PROFESSIONAL FAQs

• What is the biggest problem with blockchain?

In big public blockchains such as Bitcoin (CRYPTO:BTC) and Ethereum (CRYPTO:ETH), the scaling issue can translate into nodes demanding higher transaction fees to process transactions on the blockchain. They need to see an adequate return on their investment into computing resources.

•Which is the first block of blockchain?

The Genesis Block
The Genesis Block, also known as Block 0, is the very first block upon which additional blocks in a blockchain are added. It is effectively the ancestor that every other block can trace its lineage back to since every block references the one preceding it.

•How was blockchain created?

Blockchain has the potential to grow to be a bedrock of the worldwide record-keeping systems, but was launched just 10 years ago. It was created by the unknown persons behind the online cash currency bitcoin, under the pseudonym of Satoshi Nakamoto.

• Can blockchain be hacked?

Recently, blockchain hacks have drastically increased as hackers have discovered that vulnerabilities do in fact exist. Since 2017, public data shows that hackers have stolen around $2 billion in blockchain cryptocurrency.

• Can blockchain work without internet?

Delivering bitcoin transaction data to miners requires internet-enabled devices. As a digital currency, you cannot buy, sell or exchange bitcoin without the internet. As such, even a single day without internet access could cost bitcoin miners, exchanges, and traders millions.

•How does blockchain improve supply chains?

Blockchain can greatly improve supply chains by enabling faster and more cost-efficient delivery of products, enhancing products' traceability, improving coordination between partners, and aiding access to financing.

•Can blockchain work without cryptocurrency?

That's because when miners add a block to the bitcoin blockchain, they are rewarded with enough bitcoin to make their time and energy worthwhile. When it comes to blockchains that do not use cryptocurrency, however, miners will need to be paid or otherwise incentivized to validate transactions..

• Is blockchain different from Bitcoin?

Blockchain is the technology that underpins the cryptocurrency Bitcoin, but Bitcoin is not the only version of a blockchain distributed ledger system in the market. There are several other cryptocurrencies with their own blockchain and distributed ledger architectures.

• How are Blockchains linked?

A blockchain is “a distributed database that maintains a continuously growing list of ordered records, called blocks.” These blocks “are linked using cryptography. Each block contains a cryptographic hash of the previous block, a timestamp, and transaction data.

• Which algorithm is used with blockchain?

Blockchains generally use the SHA-256 hashing algorithm as their hash function.
